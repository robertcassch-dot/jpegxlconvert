Good to know

Modern phones produce gorgeous JPGs, but they're far from efficient. Converting JPG to JPEG XL with lossless repacking shrinks each file by around 20% while keeping it pixel-for-pixel identical to the original — you literally lose nothing and gain storage. Multiply that across a library of thousands of images and the savings add up fast.

JPEG XL is the modern, royalty-free successor to JPG: Safari already displays it natively and Chrome is rolling support out, so the format is only going to become more useful. Everything is converted privately in your browser, so you can future-proof even personal photos without uploading them anywhere.

FAQ

Does JPG to JXL lose quality?
No — lossless mode repacks the existing JPG, so it's pixel-identical and just smaller.
Where can I use .jxl?
Safari already supports it and Chrome is rolling it out; meanwhile keep your originals safe.
Is it free and private?
Yes — free, no sign-up, and the file never leaves your device.
Is the original JPG quality changed at all?
No. Lossless repacking rebuilds the same JPEG data inside the JPEG XL container, so it's mathematically identical — just smaller.
Should I delete my JPGs after converting?
Keep them until JPEG XL support is universal. For now, .jxl is great for archiving and saving space alongside your originals.
