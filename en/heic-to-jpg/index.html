Good to know

HEIC is the format the iPhone uses to save photos, and it's excellent for quality and size — until you try to open it anywhere outside Apple's world. Windows, Android, most websites and many apps simply refuse to display HEIC. Converting HEIC to JPG fixes this completely: JPG opens everywhere, so you can finally share, upload, print and email your iPhone photos without errors.

The conversion runs entirely in your browser, which matters more than usual here — these are your personal photos, and they never get uploaded to any server. Select one HEIC or your whole camera roll, choose the quality, and download standard JPGs in seconds, free and without a watermark.

FAQ

Why won't my HEIC photos open?
HEIC is Apple's format and most non-Apple apps, Windows and websites can't read it. Converting to JPG makes them open everywhere.
Will I lose quality?
JPG is lossy, but at high quality the difference is invisible for viewing, sharing and printing.
Is it free and private?
Yes — free, no sign-up, no watermark, and your photos never leave your device.
Will my photos come out rotated?
No — the converter reads the HEIC orientation data so your photos come out the right way up.
Can I convert a whole album at once?
Yes. Select as many HEIC files as you want and they're all converted together, locally on your device.
