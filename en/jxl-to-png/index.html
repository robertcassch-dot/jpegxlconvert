Good to know

Converting JPEG XL to PNG gives you a lossless image that every editor, operating system and website understands. PNG keeps 100% of the pixel data and full transparency, which makes it the safe choice for screenshots, logos, diagrams and any graphic where you can't afford even a hint of compression artefacts.

Because the whole process happens in your browser, your .jxl never leaves your computer. Drop one file or dozens and download crisp PNGs instantly — no account, no upload, no watermark. If your goal is simply to share a photo and file size matters, JPG may suit you better; for editing and perfect fidelity, PNG is the right call.

FAQ

PNG or JPG for my .jxl?
Use PNG for graphics, screenshots or transparency; JPG for photos you simply want to share.
Does it stay lossless?
Yes — PNG keeps every pixel exactly.
Is it free and private?
Yes — free, no sign-up, and the file never leaves your device.
Is PNG bigger than JPG?
Usually yes, because PNG is lossless. Choose PNG when quality and transparency matter, JPG when you want the smallest shareable file.
Will transparency be kept?
Yes — any alpha or transparency in your .jxl is fully preserved in the PNG.
