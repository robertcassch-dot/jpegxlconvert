Good to know

JPEG XL (.jxl) is a next-generation image format that delivers great quality at a fraction of the size of older formats. The catch in 2026 is support: most browsers, phones and apps still can't open a .jxl file, which is exactly why you ended up with one you couldn't view. Converting it to JPG solves that instantly — JPG is the most widely supported image format in the world and opens on every device, app and website without exception.

This converter does the whole job locally, inside your browser, using WebAssembly. Your image is never uploaded to a server, so even private or sensitive photos stay completely on your device. Drop a single .jxl or a whole batch, pick your quality, and download standard JPG files in seconds — free, with no sign-up and no watermark.

FAQ

Why won't my device open .jxl files?
Most browsers and apps haven't enabled JPEG XL yet. Converting to JPG makes the image viewable everywhere.
Will I lose quality?
JPG is lossy, but at high quality the difference is invisible for normal viewing and sharing.
Is it free and private?
Yes — free, no sign-up, no watermark, and the file never leaves your device.
Can I convert several .jxl files at once?
Yes. Drop or select as many .jxl files as you like and they're all converted in one batch, right on your device.
Does converting upload my image anywhere?
No. The conversion runs entirely in your browser with WebAssembly — nothing is ever sent to a server.
